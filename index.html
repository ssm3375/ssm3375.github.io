About

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University, working under the guidance of Dr. Jeyavijayan Rajendran in the SETH Lab. My research focuses on developing scalable and automated techniques for detecting and mitigating hardware vulnerabilities.

Research

I develop automated verification tools for detecting security vulnerabilities in hardware, including processors and System-on-Chips (SoCs). My key projects include:

  • LLM-Powered Vulnerability Detection: Building an AI framework using RAG to automatically analyze RTL hardware designs
  • SuperFuzzer: A hybrid hardware fuzzer integrating coverage-guided fuzzing, formal verification, and ML-driven strategies
  • White House Demos: Presented hardware security vulnerabilities demonstrating memory safety exploits in secure systems
