## Research Interest

Developing scalable and automated techniques for detecting and mitigating hardware vulnerabilities to streamline verification processes, enhance security, and protect critical infrastructure.

### Current Research

I am engaged in advanced research to detect and mitigate security vulnerabilities in hardware by developing robust verification tools to verify large-scale hardware, such as modern processors and System-on-Chips (SoCs). My work includes:

- Developing an LLM-powered framework leveraging Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to automatically detect and analyze vulnerabilities in RTL (Register-Transfer Level) hardware designs
- Discovering 6 vulnerabilities in the RSD RISC-V Out-of-Order Superscalar Processor
- Creating SuperFuzzer, a novel hardware fuzzer that integrates multiple fuzzing strategies for enhanced RTL vulnerability detection

## Research Experience

### TAMU Secure-and-Trustworthy-Hardware (SETH) Lab
**Adviser: Dr. Jeyavijayan Rajendran** | July 2023 – Present

- Engaged in advanced research to detect and mitigate security vulnerabilities in hardware by developing robust verification tools to verify large-scale hardware, such as modern processors and System-on-Chips (SoCs)
- Developing an LLM-powered framework, leveraging Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), to automatically detect and analyze vulnerabilities in RTL (Register-Transfer Level) hardware designs
- Discovered 6 vulnerabilities in the RSD RISC-V Out-of-Order Superscalar Processor

### SuperFuzzer
**Adviser: Dr. Jeyavijayan Rajendran** | August 2024 – May 2025

SuperFuzzer is a novel hardware fuzzer that integrates multiple fuzzing strategies, enhancing RTL vulnerability detection across diverse hardware designs. It leverages coverage feedback-guided fuzzing, formal verification, and machine-learning-driven strategies (e.g., MAB and PSO) to optimize test case generation, maximize coverage, and detect security flaws.

- Developing the SuperFuzzer framework by designing its architecture, selecting and integrating multiple fuzzing strategies, and ensuring interoperability between coverage-guided fuzzing, formal verification, and machine-learning-driven techniques to maximize vulnerability detection in complex RTL designs

### HW/SW Memory Safety Demonstrations
**Adviser: Dr. Jeyavijayan Rajendran** | May 2024 – December 2024

- Presented 3 hardware security demos to the White House, emphasizing the importance of secure hardware alongside secure software in the shift to memory-safe programming languages like Rust
- Demonstrated how hardware vulnerabilities on the Chipyard SoC could directly lead to critical software vulnerabilities
- Showcased how flaws in cache coherency mechanisms led to stack corruption on a secure program with protections built in, hijacking program flow
- Demonstrated how improper zeroization of hardware registers could leak data, enabling heap inspection attacks and compromising sensitive information
- Demonstrated how uninitialized register states propagated to the software level, enabling uninitialized memory-use exploits in software
